The Context — Why This Role Exists Now

The cost of building a bespoke internal tool has dropped to roughly the same as onboarding a SaaS product. When you factor in procurement cycles, vendor evaluations, onboarding, training, and the inevitable "we need the enterprise tier for SSO" conversation — a mid-sized SaaS adoption can represent six figures of internal time, and still deliver something that is 70% of what was actually needed.

"The best people to solve workflow and productivity challenges are the people who have to live with them every day — not the vendors who sold to procurement three years ago."

This role exists at the intersection of that economic shift and a fundamental change in how software gets made. AI-assisted development now means that a motivated individual with domain knowledge can prototype, iterate, and ship a working internal tool in hours rather than months. RMG has already demonstrated this — tools that would previously have required full project teams and procurement approval have been built in under 15 hours of human-directed AI development.

Critically, this role is not about saying "no" to teams who want to build. It is about saying "yes — and here is something so well-designed and easy to adopt that you would be crazy not to use it." Governance through quality, not bureaucracy. Standards as a consequence of usefulness, not enforcement.

Industry Signal: Gartner predicts that by 2030, 80% of large engineering teams will be reorganised into smaller, AI-augmented units. Teams integrating AI deeply into their development pipeline are reducing cycle times by 40–70% with smaller, more senior pods. The organisations that win this transition build the scaffolding — shared repos, design systems, deployment pipelines, lightweight governance — before individual and team-level tool building becomes the wild west.

Purpose of the Role

To build and lead RMG's internal engineering productivity capability — establishing the platforms, frameworks, and culture that allow individuals and teams across the organisation to rapidly design, build, and deploy bespoke internal tools. The goal is to replace low-value SaaS dependencies, streamline workflows, and surface insights that currently require expensive third-party products, while ensuring all of this happens within a coherent, governed, and sustainable architectural framework.

This is not a traditional software engineering leadership role. It sits at the intersection of product thinking, AI tooling fluency, and organisational change. Its impact is measured not by team size, but by organisational reach — how many people work differently, how much procurement cost is avoided, and how much faster the organisation can respond to its own workflow problems.

The Two Pillars

Direct Leverage

Catch the Fish — Build Once, Feed Many

Directly build the tools and solutions that should have existed years ago but never did — because the cost, complexity, or procurement overhead made them not worth attempting. This is the hands-on pillar: identifying the problems, building the solutions, and deploying them for the many.

Indirect Leverage

Teach Them to Fish — Then Give Them the Rod, the Net, the Bucket & the Licence

Provide the frameworks, components, templates, and guardrails that let any team build their own solutions safely. The rod is the framework. The net is the guardrails. The bucket is the shared platform. The licence is the empowerment to do it. Standards through enablement, not enforcement.

Key Responsibilities

1. Establish the Internal Engineering Productivity Framework

  • Define and own the internal development framework: frontend standards, shared component libraries, Azure hosting and deployment pipelines, integration patterns with BAU systems (Jira, ADO, Azure DevOps, internal APIs)
  • Leverage and extend the frontend framework being built for the customer platform — enabling internal tools to share the same design system, component library, and integration patterns, dramatically reducing time-to-value for every new tool
  • Establish internal capabilities so well-designed and easy to adopt that teams use them by choice — governance as a consequence of quality, not bureaucracy

2. Supplier and Platform Control

  • Provide a constructive alternative to teams or suppliers bypassing platform standards — instead of "don't build that," the answer is "use this internal capability, it's better, faster, and already integrated"
  • Reduce fragmentation caused by teams independently adopting overlapping SaaS tools — replace with lightweight, well-integrated internal capabilities where data integration is complex, workflows are RMG-specific, or vendor lock-in is high
  • Ensure third-party engineering teams and internal squads consume shared internal tooling, reinforcing architectural coherence without adversarial governance

3. Build and Demonstrate the Capability

  • Lead a small, senior, AI-native team actively building internal tools — and shipping them, not just designing them
  • Own a portfolio of proof-of-concept and production internal applications with measurable productivity gains, time saved, and SaaS spend avoided evidenced from day one
  • Track hours and output per project — building the internal business case iteratively as tools ship, not reconstructing it retrospectively under pressure

4. Upskill and Empower the Organisation

  • Identify high-potential individuals across Technology and beyond who have workflow problems worth solving — equip them with tools, access, and techniques to solve them themselves
  • Build internal AI-assisted development capability through hands-on co-creation, not classroom training — people leave with something working
  • Use real-world AI stories, live demos, and evidence from RMG's own portfolio to shift mindset across the organisation — the question is no longer "is this possible?" but "what is our governance model for when everyone starts doing this?"

5. Governance, Sustainability, and Environmental Responsibility

  • Establish AI model usage guidelines — the right model for the right task, avoiding unnecessary token consumption and associated cost and carbon overhead
  • Own the intersection between internal tool development and RMG's green brand commitments — "right model for the right job" is not just an economics principle at RMG, it is a brand protection requirement directly aligned with the company's position as the UK's leading green parcel and logistics carrier
  • Work with Information Security to define safe patterns for internal tool development around data access, authentication, and Azure hosting standards

Team Shape

Design principle: This is not a large organisation. It scales through replicable AI-native pods, not headcount. A typical pod is 3–5 people. Current industry thinking (Gartner, a16z, Hatchworks GenDD model) points to small, senior, AI-native pods as the optimal delivery unit. The role's leverage comes from organisational reach and framework quality — not team size.

The Broader Opportunity

This role is being created at a genuine inflection point. The organisations that build this capability now — with governance, shared standards, and a clear empowerment model — will move at a pace that procurement-led competitors simply cannot match.

At RMG, the additional dimension is responsibility. Doing this well, with appropriate model selection and energy awareness, is not just an ethical consideration — it is directly aligned with the company's position as the UK's leading green parcel and logistics carrier. The same discipline that makes this approach economically defensible makes it environmentally defensible. Those two arguments, made together, are unusually hard to argue against.

The goal is not to replace engineering. It is to make the boundary between "person with a problem" and "person who ships a solution" as thin as possible — and to build the infrastructure that makes that safe, consistent, scalable, and on-brand.

"The question to put to leadership is not 'can we do this?' That question has already been answered. The question is: what is your governance model for when individuals and teams start building their own solutions? Because that is coming whether you plan for it or not. This role builds the scaffolding before it becomes the wild west."
