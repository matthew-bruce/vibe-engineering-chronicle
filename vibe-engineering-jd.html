Role Definition · Technology & Innovation
Head of Vibe Engineering
AI-Powered Productivity & Internal Tooling
Function: Technology / Platform Reports to: Director of Platform & Build / CTO Level: Senior Individual Contributor / Head of Draft: March 2026
Why this role exists now
A structural shift is underway in how software is built and deployed. The cost and time required to create bespoke internal tooling has dropped to parity with — and in many cases below — the cost of procuring, onboarding, and maintaining third-party SaaS. This creates a new organisational opportunity: empowering the people closest to workflow and productivity problems to solve them directly, at pace, without dependency on traditional development cycles or procurement processes. This role exists to lead that movement inside the organisation.

The Opportunity

Engineering and technology organisations have long tolerated a fundamental friction: the people who experience broken workflows every day are rarely the people with the authority or tooling to fix them. They raise tickets. They wait for procurement. They accept 70% of what they asked for, at 3× the cost, after 6 months of vendor evaluation.

Vibe Engineering changes that equation. Using AI-assisted development tools — Claude Code, GitHub Copilot, and emerging agentic workflows — individuals and small teams can now build, iterate, and deploy purpose-built internal applications in days rather than quarters. The job of this role is to turn that capability into an organisational advantage: systematic, governed, and scalable.

Industry Signal
"When the cost of building a bespoke tool drops to roughly the same as onboarding a SaaS product, the entire procurement and vendor evaluation process becomes an organisational liability." The organisations that respond to this shift proactively — building scaffolding before it becomes the wild west — will gain a durable productivity advantage over those that don't.

Role Purpose

The Head of Vibe Engineering owns the organisation's internal tooling capability — championing, governing, and accelerating the use of AI-assisted development to solve real workflow and productivity problems across Technology and the wider business. This is not a traditional software engineering leadership role. It sits at the intersection of product thinking, AI tooling fluency, and organisational change.

This role is deliberately scoped to internal tooling and productivity applications, distinct from the engineering teams responsible for consumer-facing platforms and core business systems. It is the engine room for operational improvement — faster, leaner, and unconstrained by the release cadences of production software.

Key Responsibilities

Champion & Culture

  • Act as the organisation's lead advocate and practitioner for AI-assisted development — demonstrating by doing, not just directing
  • Upskill colleagues across Technology and beyond: run workshops, share tooling, build confidence in non-engineers to build for themselves
  • Maintain a living chronicle of what has been built, what has been learned, and what the measurable productivity gains have been — building the evidence base for continued investment
  • Represent the vibe engineering capability to leadership, translating technical progress into business value narratives

Delivery & Tooling

  • Identify high-value workflow and productivity problems across the organisation and prioritise them for internal tool development
  • Lead or directly contribute to the development of internal applications using AI-assisted coding tools, within a shared frontend framework and design system
  • Establish and maintain a reusable, extensible internal tooling platform — shared UI components, authentication, integration patterns with Azure, Jira, ADO, and core data sources
  • Ensure all internally-built tooling is appropriately hosted, versioned, and maintainable — not just shipped and abandoned
  • Apply model selection discipline: use the right AI tool at the right cost for each task, in alignment with the organisation's sustainability and ESG commitments

Governance & Standards

  • Define and own a lightweight governance framework for individual and team-built internal applications — code review, security baseline, data handling, deployment pipeline
  • Prevent the "wild west" scenario: establish clear standards without creating bureaucratic friction that defeats the purpose of the capability
  • Work with Security, Data, and Infrastructure teams to ensure AI-assisted development practices meet organisational policy requirements
  • Track and report on AI tooling costs, ensuring token spend and model usage remain proportionate to outcomes delivered

Strategy & Vision

  • Maintain a forward-looking view of the AI tooling landscape — surfacing relevant industry signals, capability shifts, and emerging practices for leadership
  • Develop the roadmap for the internal tooling capability: from standalone proof-of-concepts to a unified, integrated internal platform
  • Define the organisational model for vibe engineering at scale — including how pod-based delivery structures, shared infrastructure, and AI-native practices can be adopted incrementally

The Changing Shape of Engineering Teams

This role exists within a broader industry shift that is reshaping how technology organisations are structured. Understanding this context is essential for the person in this role and for the leadership team sponsoring it.

Role / Function Traditional Team (10 people) AI-Augmented Pod (3–5 people) Direction of Travel
Product Owner / Strategist 1 dedicated PO 1 — but with broader scope, closer to delivery Evolving
Business Analyst 1–2 BAs Absorbed into PO or senior dev function Diminished
Scrum Master 1 dedicated SM Shared or rotational; overhead reduced Diminished
Solutions Architect 1 SA (often external) 1 senior engineer with architectural ownership Evolving
Developers (FE/BE) 3–5 developers 1–2 agentic engineers directing AI output Evolving
DevOps 1 dedicated DevOps engineer Embedded in senior engineer; pipelines automated Evolving
QA Engineer 1–2 manual + automation QA 1 Agentic QA — AI-generated tests, human oversight New shape
Vibe Engineering Lead Did not exist New role: owns AI tooling, governance, upskilling Net new

Sources: Gartner (80% of large engineering teams reorganised into smaller AI-augmented units by 2030), HatchWorks GenDD research, Optimum Partners Engineering Management 2026 report.

Important Nuance
The move to smaller teams is not without risk. Deloitte's 2025 Tech Value Survey found that teams reporting the strongest AI outcomes were not the smallest — they were the most cognitively diverse and well-connected. The "senior-only" hiring model creates a talent hollow by removing the entry-level career ladder. The right model is smaller and more senior, with deliberate investment in bringing newer talent up through AI-native practices rather than eliminating them entirely.

Person Specification

This role does not require a traditional software engineering background. It requires a rare combination of practical AI fluency, product and systems thinking, and the communication skills to shift organisational behaviour. The best candidate may be someone who has been frustrated for years by the gap between the problems they see and the tools available to solve them.

AI Tooling Fluency

Hands-on experience with AI-assisted development tools (Claude Code, Copilot, Cursor or similar). Has shipped something real. Understands the difference between the right model and the expensive model.

Product Thinking

Instinctively frames problems as user needs before jumping to solutions. Can distinguish between a workflow problem worth solving and one better left alone. Comfortable with ambiguity and iteration.

Systems & Integration Awareness

Understands enough about APIs, authentication, data flows, and cloud infrastructure (Azure) to make sensible architectural decisions. Does not need to be a hands-on engineer but must be a credible technical partner.

Communication & Influence

Can translate technical capability into business outcomes for non-technical audiences. Comfortable presenting to senior stakeholders. Able to build advocates across the organisation — not just in Technology.

Governance Mindset

Understands that freedom without structure creates risk. Can design lightweight governance that enables rather than obstructs. Naturally thinks about security, sustainability, and cost alongside velocity.

Credibility Through Doing

Has evidence of building things. The strongest candidates will have a portfolio of internal tools, prototypes, or AI-assisted projects — not just opinions about what's possible.

What Success Looks Like

At 3 months

  • At least two internal tools in active use, measurably replacing a manual process or third-party tool dependency
  • A shared frontend framework and deployment pipeline established for internal tooling
  • A lightweight governance model documented and socialised with Security and Infrastructure
  • A growing community of internal vibe engineers across Technology — people who have been upskilled and are building for themselves

At 12 months

  • A portfolio of 8–12 internal applications in production, collectively evidencing significant productivity gains
  • A quantified business case: time saved, procurement costs avoided, workflow improvements measured
  • A coherent internal platform strategy — individual apps beginning to integrate under a unified data and identity layer
  • The organisation's AI tooling costs are tracked, governed, and proportionate — aligned with ESG commitments
  • Leadership regularly referencing internal tooling capability as a competitive differentiator
A Note for the CTO
This role is the organisational answer to a question that is coming whether you plan for it or not: "What happens when individuals and teams start building their own solutions?" The choice is not whether this happens — it is whether it happens in a governed, strategic way that builds a durable capability, or whether it becomes fragmented and ungoverned. The Head of Vibe Engineering builds the scaffolding before it becomes the wild west.
